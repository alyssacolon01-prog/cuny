At Manhattan CUNY, There Was a Rise in Higher Education Graduates. What's Driving Students Back?

In a New York City Higher Education report, student enrollment at Manhattan CUNY increased from 2020 through 2023.

By Alyssa Colon

December 1, 2025

In New York City, there are 25 public CUNY’S campuses across all five boroughs, with a degree being the most affordable. CUNY’s four-year degree program may not be as cheap as a two-year degree, costing$3,465 per semester for full-time New York State residents. We still saw a spike in student enrollment in higher education, especially after the COVID-19 lockdown was lifted. The reason for the highr spike in graduates is due to financial help.

Due to the lockdown, not only CUNY but also SUNY responded to the pandemic by helping students financially. They distributed a combined $1.4 billion in emergency grants to students from three federal stimulus bills: the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act, the Coronavirus Response and Relief Supplemental Appropriations (CRRSA) Act, and the American Rescue Plan (ARP) Act.

“At a time when the value of higher education is being scrutinized,” said CUNY Chancellor Félix V. Matos Rodríguez. “The expansion of New York State’s generous financial aid programs recognizes the importance of affordability in encouraging New Yorkers to pursue their educational and career goals.”
